All About English

Learn the intricacies of English grammar and usage

In English grammar, the subject is the part of a sentence or clause that commonly indicates (a) what it is about, or (b) who or what performs the action (that is, the agent).

Present Simple

The simple present tense is used to describe habits, unchanging situations, general truths, and fixed arrangements. Examples:

  • The sun rises in the east.
  • He goes to school every day.
  • Water boils at 100°C.

Past Continuous

The past continuous tense is used to describe actions that were ongoing at a specific point in the past. Examples:

  • She was reading a book when the phone rang.
  • They were playing football at 5 PM yesterday.
  • I was cooking dinner when he arrived.

Future Perfect

The future perfect tense is used to describe an action that will be completed before a specified point in the future. Examples:

  • By this time next year, I will have graduated from college.
  • She will have finished her work by 6 PM.
  • They will have built the house by the end of the month.
